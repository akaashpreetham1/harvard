Akaash Preetham

Section B · International · First-gen · Chennai, India
"Who am I? Depends on who you ask. A son to loving parents, an annoying brother, a South Indian to North Indians, an Indian to non-Indians. To you, hopefully a friend."

Akaash grew up in Chennai, India, and was the first in his family to cross an ocean. He packed his life into two suitcases and traveled halfway across the world — leaving important people behind — to build something new. He cares deeply about representing diverse voices, and believes that the people who've had to fight hardest to get here understand best what HBS should be.

Dimitri Henry

Section J · Dual Degree (HBS + HKS) · Flemington, NJ
"First in his family to visit Felipe's. Driven by a desire to advance positive systemic change and uplift marginalized communities — one burrito bowl at a time."

Dimitri grew up in Flemington, NJ, and came to Harvard with a clear mission: make systems work better for people who've been left out of them. As a dual degree candidate splitting his time between HBS and the Harvard Kennedy School, he's lived the power — and the friction — of bridging two worlds. He's seen firsthand what becomes possible when you cross the metaphorical river.

What Unites Them

Same starting line. Same destination.

01
First-generation everything

One crossed an ocean. One was first in his family to visit Felipe's. Both know what it means to build from scratch — and why that perspective matters in leadership.

02
Section Presidents who get it

They've both served as section presidents. They know the administration, the bottlenecks, and the small things that make or break a week. That's not a coincidence — it's a qualification.

03

Before writing a single policy, they asked. They'll keep asking. If there's something you want to raise — reach out directly. That's the job.
